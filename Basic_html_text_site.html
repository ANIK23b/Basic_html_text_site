Eight edition of Women's World Cup

On June 7, 2019, the world cup will kick off with hosts France taking on South Korea. The final will be held on July 7, 2019.

History of Women's World Cup

The FIFA Women's World Cup was founded in 1991. It has only been around for less than 25 years. The first WWC tournament was held 61 years after the men's first FIFA World Cup in 1930.



Hosts & Winners

China was the first country to host the WWC tournament. Since then, it has been held in Sweden, Germany, Canada, twice in China and the US, and is now being held in France. The first FIFA Women's World Cup was won by the United States. The United States holds the most championship titles with three wins.



Facts & Popularity

During the 2007 WWC in China, US captain Kristine Lilly competed in her fifth and final tournament, which made her the first woman, and one of three players in history to appear in five world cups. Australian Ellyse Perry has appeared in both the women's cricket (2009) and football World Cup (2011). The 2015 FIFA World Cup featured the youngest coach in World Cup history - Ecuador's Vanessa Arauz, at 26 years. In 2015, 750 million people tuned in to watch FIFA women's world cup, held in Canada.
